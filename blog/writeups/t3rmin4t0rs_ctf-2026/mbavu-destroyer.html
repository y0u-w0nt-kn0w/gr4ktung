🧠 Understanding the Vulnerability

RSA security fundamentally depends on the computational difficulty of factoring large numbers:

$n = p \times q$

However, in this challenge, 🚨 the primes $p$ and $q$ are already known. This completely breaks RSA, because knowing the primes allows us to effortlessly reconstruct the private key.

🔑 RSA Decryption Theory

With $p$ and $q$ in hand, we can follow standard RSA key generation steps to decrypt the message.

  1. Compute Euler's Totient:
    $\phi(n) = (p - 1)(q - 1)$
  2. Compute Private Exponent:
    $d \equiv e^{-1} \pmod{\phi(n)}$
  3. Decrypt Ciphertext:
    $m = c^d \bmod n$
  4. Convert to Readable Text:
    The decrypted integer ($m$) is converted back into bytes to recover the flag.
